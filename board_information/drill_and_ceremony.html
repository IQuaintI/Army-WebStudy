What is the purpose of drill?

Drill enables commanders to quickly move their forces from one point to another, amass their forces into a battle formation that affords maximum firepower, and maneuver those forces as the situation develops

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is alignment?

The arrangement of several elements on the same line

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is cover?

Aligning oneself directly behind the person to one's immediate front while maintaining correct distances

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is distance?

The space between elements when the elements are one behind the other. Between units, it varies with the size of the formation. Between Soldiers it is an arms length to the front plus six inches or about 36 inches - measured from the chest of one Soldier to the back of the Soldier immediately in front

In regards to drill and ceremony, define an element?

An individual, squad, section, platoon, company, or larger unit forming the part of the next higher unit

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is a file?

A column that has a front of one element

What is a flank?

The right or left side of any formation as observed by an element within that formation

What is a formation?

The arrangement of elements of a unit in a prescribed manner

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is a line?

A formation in which the elements are side-by-side or abreast of each other

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is a column?

A formation in which the elements are one behind the other

How do you change a line formation to a column formation?

To change a line formation to a column formation, the command is Right Face

To change a column formation to a line formation, the command is Left Face

How many types of intervals are there?

Three

What are the three types of interval?

1. Close interval
2. Double interval
3. Normal interval

What is close inteval?

Lateral space between Soldiers, measured from right to left by the Soldier on the right. The heel of the left hand is placed on the hip, even with the belt line, fingers and thumbs extended and facing downward, with the elbow in line with the body and touching the arm of the Soldier to their left

What is double interval?

Lateral space between Soldiers, measured from right to left by raising both arms shoulder high with fingers extended and joined (palms facing down) so that the fingertips are touching the fingerips of the Soldier to their left and right

What is normal interal?

Lateral space between Soldiers, measured from right to left by the Soldier on the right extending their left arm shoulder high, fingers and thumbs extended and joined, with the middle finger touching the right shoulder of the Soldier to the left

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is post?

The correct pace for an officer or noncommisioned officer to stand in a prescribed formation

In regards to drill and ceremony, what is a rank?

A line that is one element in depth

What is a step?

The prescribed distance measured from one heel to the heel of the marching Soldier

What is a two-part command?

A command having a prepatory command and a command of execution (Prepatory command: "Right" / Command of execution: "Face")

What is a combined command?

The prepatory command and a command of execution used without pause ("Fall in")

What is a supplementary command?

Oral orders given by a subordinate leader that reinforces and complements a commander's order

In regards to drill and ceremony, what are directives?

Oral orders given by a commander that directs or causes a subordinate leader to take action (Telling your subordinate to have their unit fall in)

What position should a Soldier be at when addressing an officer?

How did the hand salute originate?

The salute dates back to Roman times. When a knight would approach another in peace, the visor would be lifted with the right hand. This idea has carried into the modern era via hand salute
