What are the two types of authority?

Command and general military authority

Where does command authority come from?

Policy, procedure, and precedents

Where does general military authority come from?

Oaths of office, laws, rank structure, traditions, and regulation

Define general military authority

Authority extended to all Soldiers to take action and act in the absence of a unit leader or other designated authority

Define command authority

The authority a commander lawfully exercises over subordinates by virtue of rank or assignment

Where do NCOs obtain their authority?

From the Secretary of the Army as outlined within Army regulation

How is military authority exercised?

Promptly, fairly, courteosly, and firmly

Leaders may delegate any or all of their authority to their subordinates unless __?

When restricted by law or a superior. A leader cannot delegate authority they do not have

What is a delegation of authority?

The passing on of certain aspects of a mission or task to the appropriate level capable of handling the task or mission. This authority may be further delegated if need be

What is one of the most effective measure to correct substandard performance?

Corrective training

Define "On the spot" correction

A tactful correction usullay required for 1 of 2 reasons: The Soldier does not know the standard or the Soldier knowingly violated the standard. Some examples are: Directing a Soldier to get a haircut or to take their hands out of their pockets

Define corrective training

Extra training and instructions are designed to correct deficiencies and eliminate the need for formal disciplinary measures in the future. It cannot be used as a punitive measure, you must distinguish it from punitive measures or even the appearance of punitive measures, and the Soldier will only receive it for as long as the deficiency exists

What are the rules for corrective training?

1. Must be given to correct deficiencies
2. Must be directly related to the deficiency
3. Be oriented to improve the Soldier's performance
4. may not be punishment
5. Can be conducted on or off-duty
6. Should continue until the deficiency is corrected

What does a good counseling do?

It informs the Soldier on what they are doing well, what they are doing wrong, and how to improve on their weaknesses

What leader activity is central to leader development?

Define counseling

The process used by leaders to review and guide improvement in a subordinate's demonstrated performance and potential

How many types of developmental counselings are there?

Three

What are the three types of developmental counseling?

1. Event counseling
2. Performance counseling
3. Professional growth counseling

Define event counseling

Involves a specific event or situation

What are some examples of event-oriented counseling?

Superior or subordinate performance, reception and integration, crisis, refereal, promotion, and separation

Define performance counseling

When leaders conduct a review of a subordinate's duty performance over a certain period of time

What does a performance counseling include?

1. Discussion of established performance objectives and standards for the next period
2. Periodic performance counseling as a part of the NCOER support form requirement
3. Beginning of and during the evaluation period and provides opportunity for leaders to establish and clarify expected values, attributes, and competencies

Define professional growth counseling

A discussion designed to further the growth of an individual in their career

What does a professional growth counseling include?

1. Planning for accomplishment of individual and professional goals
2. Identify and discuss subordinate's strengths and weaknesses
3. Create an individual development plan that builds on those strengths and weaknesses
4. Opportunities for civilian and military schooling, future assignments, special programs, and reenlistment options

Why do leaders counsel?

What are the three types of duties an NCO has?

1. Specified duties
2. Directed duties
3. Implied duties

Define specified duties

Those related to jobs and positions (MOS related duties, Army regulation, etc.)

Define directed duties

Those issued by superiors orally or in writing (CQ, Staff duty, Key control NCO, etc)

Define implied duties

Often support specified duties but in some cases may not be related to MOS positions. May not be written but implied instructions. Duties usually depend on individual initiative (improve quality of life, keeps unit functioning at optimum level, etc.)

What are your roles as a leader?

1. Trainer
2. Disciplinarian
3. Coach
4. Leader

Define leadership

The process of influencing people by providing purpose, direction, and motivation to accomplish the mission and improve the organization

How many roles does an NCO have and what are they?

The six roles are: 1. Convey information and provide day to day guidance
2. Train Soldiers to cope, prepare, and perform regardless of the situation
3. Set and maintain high quality standards
4. Be the standard bearer and role model critical thinking, educating, and developing subordinates
5. Prepare Soldiers for missions, stressing trade-crafts, and physical hardening
6. Coach, counsel, and mentor
