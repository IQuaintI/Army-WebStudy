What effects on the forces does every change in doctrine have?

The Army must disseminate the change to the force. Operating forces must study and adopt the change. Soldiers and leaders must understand what has been rescinded from doctrine. It takes time for a change to permeate the force.

What are techniques?

Non-prescriptive ways and methods used to perform missions, functions, or tasks.

Defined tactics.

The employments and ordered arrangement of forces in relation to each other.

Define a mission.

The tasks, together with a purpose, that clearly indicates the action to be taken and the reason therefore.

What is a mission statement?

A short sentence that describes an organization's essential task purpose and action containing the elements of who, what, when, where, and why.

What is an operations order?

A directive issued by a commander to a subordinate commander for the purpose of effecting the coordinated execution of an operation.

What are mission orders?

Directives that emphasize to subordinates the results that are desired but not how they are to be achieved.

What is the definition of joint doctrine?

Fundamental principles that guide the employment of the United States military forces in coordinated actions towards a common objective and may include tactics, techniques, and procedures.

What are concepts?

Ideas for a significant change based on proposed new approaches to the conduct of operations or technology.
