Define Special Operations.

Operations requiring unique modes of employment, tactical techniques, equipment and training often conducted in hostile, denied or politically sensitive environments and characterized by one or more of the following.

What are the two core competencies of special operations forces?

Special warfare and surgical strike.

What is a surgical strike?

The execution of activities in a precise manner that employ special operations forces in hostile, denied or politically sensitive environments or seize, destroy, capture, exploit, recover or damage designated targets or influence threats.

Define special warfare.

The execution of activities involves a combination of lethal and non-lethal actions taken by a specially trained and educated force that has a deep understanding of cultures and foreign language, proficiency in small unit tactics and the ability to build and fight alongside indigenous combat formations in a permissive and certain or hostile environment.

Are SOF units a substitute for conventional forces?

No.

Define unconventional warfare.

Activities conducted to enable a resistance movement or insurgency to coerce disrupted overthrow of government or occupy power by operating through or with an underground auxiliary or guerrilla force in a denied area.

Name the special operations imperatives

understanding the operational environment, recognizing political implications, facilitate interagency activities, engage with threats discriminately, anticipate long term.

What is foreign internal defense?

Participation by civil and military agencies of a government in any of the action programs taken by another pro-government or other designated organization to free and protect the society from subversion, lawlessness, insurgency, terrorism, and other threats.

Define security force assistance.

The Department of Defense's activities that contribute to unified action by the US government to support the development of the capacity and capability of foreign security forces and their supporting institutions.

What is a special operations task force?

A temporary or semi-permanent grouping of Army special operations units under one commander and formed to carry out a specific operation or continuing mission.

What is an advanced operations base?

A small temporary base established near earth in a joint operations area to command control and support special operations training or tactical operations.

What is a guerrilla force?

A group of irregular predominantly indigenous personnel organized along military lines to conduct military and paramilitary operations in enemy held hostile or denied territory.

Define irregular warfare.

A violent struggle among state and non-state actors for legitimacy and influence over the relevant population. What is subversion? Actions determined to undermine the military, economic, philosophical or political strength or morale of a governing body.

Define resistance movement.

An organized, desired effort by some portion of the civil population of a country to resist the legally established government or an occupying power to disrupt civil order instability.

Define underground.

A cellular covert element with unconventional warfare that is compartmentalized and conducts covert or clandestine forces in areas normally denied to the auxiliary in the guerrilla force.

Define auxiliary.

The support elements of the irregular organization whose organization and operations are clandestine in nature and whose members do not openly indicate their sympathy or involvement with their irregular movement.
