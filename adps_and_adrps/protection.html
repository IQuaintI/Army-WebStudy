Define protection

The preservation of the effectiveness and survivability of mission-related, military and non-military personnel, equipment, facilities, information and infrastructure deployed or located within or outside the boundaries of a given operational area.

What is the first step towards effective protection?

Planning

Define fratricide,

The unintentional killing or wounding a friendly or neutral personnel by friendly firepower.

What is usually the cause of fratricide?

An error, by a leader or soldier.

Define CBRN,

Chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear.

What is risk management?

The process of identifying, assessing, and controlling risks that arise from operational factors and making decisions that balance risk costs with mission benefits.

Define measures of effectiveness,

A criterion used to assess changes in the system's behavior capabilities or operational environments that is tied to measuring the attainment of an end-state achievement or an object in creation of an effect.

Define operational area security.

Formal security operations conducted to protect friendly forces, installations, routes, and actions within an area of operation.

Define base defense.

The local military measures both normal and emergency required to nullify or reduce the effectiveness of enemy attacks on or sabotage of a base to ensure that the maximum capacity of its facilities is available to U.S. forces.

Define high-risk personnel.

Personnel who by their great assignment, symbolic value, or relative isolation are likely to be attractive or accessible to terrorist targets.

Define convoy security operation.

A specialized kind of area security operations conducted to protect convoys.

Define operation security

A process of identifying critical information and subsequently analyzing friendly actions, attendance to military operations, and other activities.

What does physical security consist of?

Physical measures that are designed to safeguard personnel to prevent unauthorized access to equipment, installations, materials, and documents, and to safeguard them against espionage, sabotage, damage, and theft.

What does EOD and what does this role?

Explosive ordnance disposal. Exists to eliminate or reduce the effectiveness of ordnance and hazards to protect combat power.

Define resettlements.

The quartering of a population or group for their protection.

Define a hazard.

A condition with the potential to cause injury, illness, or death of personnel, damage or loss to equipment or property or mission, degradation.
