What is the Army Universal Task List?

A comprehensive but not all-inclusive listing of Army tasks, missions, and operations.

Define mobilization.

The process by which Army tactical forces or parts of the forces are brought to a state of readiness for war or other national emergencies.

What is an Envelopment?

A form of maneuver in which an attacking force seeks to avoid the principal enemy defenses by seizing objectives behind those defenses that allow the targeted enemy force to be destroyed in their current positions.

What is a frontal attack?

A form of maneuver in which an attacking force seeks to destroy a weaker enemy force or fix a larger force in place over a broad front.

Define PMCS.

Preventative Maintenance Checks and Services.

What is an airborne assault?

The use of airborne forces to parachute into an objective area or attack or eliminate arms, resistance, and secure designed, designated objectives from which to conduct lodging activities which receives key terrain to block or tonight's use by the enemy.

What is an encirclement operation?

Operations where one force loses its freedom to maneuver because an opposing force is able to isolate it by controlling all ground lines of communication and reinforcement.

What is a turning movement?

A form of maneuver in which the attacking force seeks to avoid the enemy's principal defensive position by seizing objectives already wrote all the shit down so this doesn't matter.

What is infiltration?

A form of maneuver in which an attacking force conducts undetected movement through or into an area occupied by enemy forces to occupy a position of advantage behind those enemy position lines while exposing only small elements to the enemy defensive fires.

What is a penetration?

A form of maneuver in which an attacking front seeks to rupture enemy defenses on a narrow front to disrupt defense systems.

What is a tactical road march?

A rapid movement used to relocate units within an area of operations and to prepare for combat operations.

What is a battle position?

A defensive location oriented on a likely enemy avenue of approach.

What is a flank attack?

A form of offensive maneuver directed at the flank of an enemy.

What is a cordon and search?

A technique of conducting a movement to contact and involves isolating a target area and searching suspected locations within the target area or in capture to ensure possible enemy forces in contraband.

What is an exfiltration?

To conduct by which the commander removes personnel or units from an area under enemy control by stealth, military, deception, surprise, or clandestine means.

What does it mean to canonize enemy movement?

When the commander restricts enemy movement to a narrow area by exploiting terrain coupled by the use of obstacles, fires, or friendly maneuvers.
