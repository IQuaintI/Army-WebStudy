Sustainment (ADRP 4-0, ADP 4-0)

Home

Define sustainment.

The provisions of logistics, personnel, services, and health service support necessary to maintain operations until successful mission completion.

Define logistics.

Planning and executing of the movement and supporting of forces.

Define survivability.

All aspects of protecting personnel, weapons, and supplies while simultaneously deceiving the enemy.

What is the primary purpose of army pre-positioned stocks?

To reduce the initial strategic lift required to support a force projection of the army.

What is improvisation?

The ability to adapt circumstance, sustainment operations to unexpected situations or circumstances affecting the mission.

What does USAMC stand for?

The US Army Material Command.

What does LCMC stand for?

Lifecycle Management Command.

What is theater closing?

The process of redeploying army forces and equipment from a theater and draw down and removal or disposition of army non-unit equipment and materials and the transition of materials and facilities back to host nations of civil authorities.

What agency is in charge of sustainment support to joint forces during peace and war?

The defense logistics agency.

What command provides common user and commercial air land and air sea transportation terminal management area refueling to support deployment, sustainment and redeployment.

US transportation command.

Define maintenance.

All actions taken to retain materials and serviceable conditions or to return it to serviceability.

What is the brigade support battalion?

An organic competition of BCT, fires and maneuver enhancement brigades.

What name the ten classes of supply?

Class 1. Subsistence including health and welfare items
Class 2. Clothing, individual equipments, tool sets
Class 3. POL, petroleum and solid fuels including bulk and packaged fuels lubricating oils and lubricants
Class 4. Construction materials
Class 5. Ammunition
Class 6. Personnel demanded items, non-military use
Class 7. Major items, launchers, tanks, mobile, machine shops, vehicles
Class 8. Medical material including medical peculiar repair parts
Class 9. Repair parts and components including kit assemblies and sub-assemblies repairable and non-repairables required for maintaining support for all equipment
Class 10. Material support non-military programs such as agriculture and economic development not included in classes 1 through 9.

Define stewardship.

A careful and responsible management of resources entrusted to the government in order to execute responsible governance.

Who is responsible for port opening and operations?

The United States military surface deployment and distribution command.

Who is responsible for integrating balancing and providing reach back to the sustainment base?

The Army, fuel, support, brigades, the AFSBs.

What does TEC stand for?

Theater engineer command.

What is host nation support?

Civil and military assistance rendered by a nation to foreign forces within its territory during peace time crisis of emergencies or war based on agreements mutually concluded between nations.

What is the task of medical units?

To triage casualties, treat and return to duty or resuscitate and stabilize for evacuations the next high role of medical care or out of the theater of operations.

Define contract law.

The application of domestic and international law to acquisition of good services and construction.

Define fiscal law.

The application of domestic statuses and regulations to the funding of military operations and support to non-federal agencies and organizations.

What would fail to provide sustainment cause?

A pause or cumulation of an operation resulting in the loss of initiative.

Define retrograde of material.

The return of material from the owning and using unit back to the distribution system to the source and supply directed ship to location and or point of disposal.

What does ADACG stand for?

Arrival departure airfield control group.

What does APOD stand for?

Aerial ports of the debarkation.

What does ARFORGEN stand for?

Army forces generation.

What does DFAS stand for?

Defense finances and accounting services.

What does HBCT stand for?

Heavy brigade combat team.
