Operations Process (ADRP 5-0, ADP 5-0)

Home

What are the four major mission command activities of the operations process?

Plan, Prepare, Execute, Assess.

What does CCIR stand for?

Commander's Critical Information Requirements.

What are the two components of CCIR?

Friendly Force Information Requirements and Priority Intelligence Requirements.

What does EEFI stand for?

Essential Elements of Friendly Information.

Name three methodologies that assist commanders and staffs with planning.

Army Design Methodology, Military Decision Making Process, Troop Leading Procedures.

Name the steps in the troop leading procedures.

1. Receive the mission
2. Issue a warning order
3. Make a tentative plan
4. Initiate movement
5. Conduct reconnaissance
6. Complete the plan
7. Issue the order
8. Supervise and refine the plan

Name the principles for the effective use of the operations process.

1. Commanders drive the operations process
2. Build and maintain situational understanding
3. Apply critical and creative thinking
4. Encourage collaboration and dialogue

Define Planning.

The art and science of understanding a situation and envisioning a desired future and laying out effective ways to bring about that future.

Name the steps of the MDMP process.

1. Receipt of mission
2. Mission analysis
3. Course of action development
4. Course of action analysis
5. Course of action comparison
6. Course of action approval
7. Order of production, dissemination, and transition.

Define commander's visualization.

The mental process of developing situational understanding, determining a desired end state, and envisioning an operational approach by which the force will achieve that end state.

What are the four ways commanders express their visualizations?

1. Commander's intent
2. Planning guidance
3. Including an operational approach
4. Commander's critical information requirements
5. Essential elements of friendly information.

What is situational understanding?

The product of applying analysis and judgment to relevant information to determine the relationships among the operational and mission variables to facilitate decision making.

What is a running estimate?

The continuous assessment of the current situation used to determine if the current operation is proceeding according to the commander's intent and if the planning future operations are supportable.

What is a priority intelligence requirement?

An intelligence requirement stated as a priority for intelligence support that the commander and staff need to understand the adversary or the operational environment.

What is a friendly force information requirement?

Information the commanders and staff need to understand the status and friendly forces and supporting capabilities.

What is an essential element of friendly information?

A critical aspect of friendly operation that if known to the enemy will subsequently compromise, lead to failure, or limit success of the operation, therefore should be protected from enemy detection.

What is the liaison?

That contact or intercommunication to maintain between elements of military forces or other agencies to ensure mutual understanding and unity of purpose and action.

What are the four planning pitfalls?

1. Attempting to forecast and dictate events to far into the future
2. Turning to plan in too much detail
3. Using the plan as a script or execution
4. Institutionalizing rigid planning methods.

What is a confirmation brief?

A briefing subordinate leaders give to the higher commander immediately after the operation order is given.

What is a rehearsal?

A session in which the commander and staff for unit practices expected actions to improve performance during execution.

What does ISR stand for?

Intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance.

What integration process do commanders and staffs use to synchronize specific functions throughout the operations process?

Intelligence preparation of the battlefield, targeting, and risk assessment.

What are the four steps of intelligence prep for the battle?

1. Define the operational environment
2. Describe environmental effects of operations.
3. Evaluate the threat to determine the course of action

What is terrain management?

The process of allocating terrain by establishing areas of operation designating assembly areas and specifying locations for units and activities to de-conflict activities that man for each other.

What does ADCON stand for?

Administrative control.

What does ASCOP stand for?

Areas, structures, capabilities, organizations, people, and events.

What does MOE stand for?

Measures of effectiveness.

What does AOPCON stand for?

Operational control.

What does OPLAN stand for?

Operational plan.

What does TACON stand for?

Tactical control.

Define battle rhythm.

A deliberate daily cycle of command staff and unit activities intended to synchronize current and future operations.

Define organic as it relates to the unit's organizations.

Those parts of a unit listed in its table of organizations.

Define general support.

That support, which is given to the supported force as a whole and not to any particular subdivision thereof.

Define priority of support.

A priority separate the commander to ensure a subordinate unit has a support in accordance with its relative importance to accomplish the mission.

What does direct support?

A support relationship requiring a force to support another specific force and authorizing it to answer directly to the supported force's request for assistance.
