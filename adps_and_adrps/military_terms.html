What are the six principles of mission command?

1. Build cohesive teams through mutual trust
2. Create shared understanding
3. Provide a clear commander's intent
4. Exercise disciplined initiative
5. Use the mission orders
6. Accept prudent risk

What is the Army's operational concept?

Unified land operations.

What is the mutual trust?

Shared confidence among commanders, subordinates, and partners.

What is the disciplined initiative?

Action in the absence of orders when existing orders no longer fit the situation or when unforeseen opportunities or threats arise.

Define mission orders.

Directives that emphasize to subordinates the results to be attained, not how to achieve them.

Who are the central figures in mission command?

Commanders.

Who supports the commanders in exercises of mission command?

The staff.

What does the ATTP stand for?

Army tactics, techniques and procedures.

Define mission command.

The exercise of authority and direction by the commander using mission orders to enable disciplined initiative within the commander's intent to empower agile and adaptive leaders in the conduct of unified land operations.

What must an effective approach to mission command be?

Comprehensive without being rigid.

What three ideas does mission command incorporate?

1. The exercise of mission command
2. The mission command philosophy
3. The mission command war-fighting function

What is the exercise of mission command based on?

Mutual trust, shared understanding, and purpose.

Define unified action partners.

Those military forces, governmental and non-governmental organizations, and elements of the private sector with whom the armed forces plan, coordinate, synchronize and integrate during the conduct of operations.

Define authority.

The delegated power to judge, act or command.

What is the mission command war-fighting function?

The related tasks and systems that develop and integrate those activities enabling a commander to balance the art of command and the science of control in order to integrate the other war fighting functions

Define primary staff tasks.

1. Conduct the operations process: plan, prepare, execute and asses
2. Conduct knowledge management and information management
3. Synchronize information related capabilities
4. Conduct cyber electromagnetic activities

Define mission command system.

The arrangement of personnel, networks, information systems, processes and procedures and facilities and equipment that enable commanders to conduct operations.

What does a commander's mission command begin with?

People

Define information system.

Equipment that collects, processes, stores, displays and disseminates information. This includes computers, hardware and software and communications as well as policies and procedures for their use.

Define the science of control.

The science and procedures used to improve the commander's understanding and support accomplishing missions.

What are the five additional commander-led and staff-supported tasks?

1. Conduct military deception
2. Conduct civil affairs operations
3. Install, operate, and maintain the network
4. Conduct airspace control
5. Conduct information protection

Define command.

The authority that a commander in the armed forces lawfully exercises over subordinates by virtue of rank or assignment.

Define graphics control measure.

The symbol used on maps and displays to regulate forces and war-fighting functions.

What is military deception?

Actions executed to deliberately mislead adversary military decision makers as to friendly military capabilities, intentions, and operations, thereby causing the adversaries to take specific actions that will contribute to the accomplishment of the mission.

Who is the senior non-commissioned officer that assists at battalion and higher levels.

Command sergeant major

What are the staff's three primary functions?

1. Supporting the commander
2. Assisting subordinate units
3. Informing units and organizations outside the headquarters

What is the staff's most important function?

To support and advise the commander through planning, preparation, execution, and assessment.

What are military operations?

Complex human endeavors characterized by the continuous mutual adaptation of give and take, moves, and countermoves among all participants.

What are the commander's three primary mission command and war-fighting function tasks?

1. Drive the operations process through their activities of understanding, visualizing, describing, directing, leading, and assessing operations.
2. Develop teams, both with their own organizations and with joint interagency and multinational partners
3. Inform and influence audiences inside and outside their organization

What is the art order of command comprised of?

1. Authority

What is responsibility?

The obligation to carry forward an assigned tasks to a successful conclusion.

What do commanders apply their judgment towards?

Identify, accept, and mitigate risk while prioritizing resources and delegating authority.

What is the first consideration for allocating resources?

How are these contributions effective to completing the mission.

What does the COP stand for?

Common operational picture.

Define common operational and picture.

A single display of relevant information about a commander's area of interest tailored to the user's requirement and based on common data and information shared by more than one command.

What are two types of control?

Positive and procedural.

What is positive control?

Techniques for actively regulating forces that require explicit coordination with the commander's and supporting leaders.

What is procedural control?

Techniques of regulating forces or actions that are governed by written or oral instructions that do not require authorization to execute.

What is knowledge from the management?

The process of enabling knowledge flow to enhance shared understanding and learning and decision-making.

The fine information management

The science of using procedures and information science systems to collect, process, store, display, disseminate, and protect data, information and knowledge.

What are cyber electromagnetic activities?

Activities leveraged to seize, retain, exploit, and advantage over adversaries and enemies in both cyberspace and the electromagnetic spectrum, while simultaneously denying and degrading adversary and enemy use of the same and protecting mission command systems.

Define authority.

The delegated power to judge, act, or command.

Define control.

The regulation of forces and war-fighting functions to accomplish the mission in accordance with commander's intent.

Define information protection.

Active or passive measures to safeguard and defend friendly information and information systems.

Define information requirements.

Any information element the commander and staff require to accomplish the operations.

Define mission orders.

Directives that emphasize this award and its results to be attained and how to achieve them.

What is unified actions.

The synchronization, coordination, and/or integration of the activities of governmental and non-governmental entities with military operations to achieve unity of effort.

Define unity of effort.

The coordination and cooperation towards common objectives even if participants are not necessarily part of the same command or organization in the product of successful unified action.

What are the considerations for prioritizing resources?

1. Effectively accomplish the mission while conserving resources
2. Protect the lives of soldiers
3. Apply the principles of mass and economy of force
4. Posture the forces of subsequent operations

Name the considerations for determining degree of control.
